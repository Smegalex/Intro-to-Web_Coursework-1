Wildlife of North America

Bald Eagle

Haliaeetus leucocephalus

The bald eagle is a majestic bird of prey and the national symbol of the United States. Known for its striking white head and tail contrasting with a dark brown body, the bald eagle is an iconic species found primarily near large bodies of water where it hunts fish and waterfowl. With powerful talons and keen eyesight, it's a formidable predator and a symbol of strength and freedom.

Grizzly Bear

Ursus arctos horribilis

The grizzly bear is a large and powerful carnivore found in North America, primarily in the forested regions of Alaska and western Canada. Known for its distinctive hump on its shoulders and its silver-tipped fur, the grizzly bear is an apex predator that primarily feeds on berries, fish, and small mammals. While they are mostly solitary animals, grizzly bears can be territorial and formidable when defending their food or offspring.

Raccoon

Procyon lotor

Raccoons are highly adaptable mammals found throughout North America, from urban environments to forests and wetlands. Known for their distinctive facial mask and ringed tail, raccoons are omnivorous and opportunistic feeders, consuming a wide range of foods such as fruits, nuts, insects, small mammals, and even human refuse.

American Bison

Bison bison

The American bison, also known as the buffalo, is an iconic symbol of North America's prairies and grasslands. Once numbering in the millions, bison were nearly driven to extinction by hunting and habitat loss but have since made a remarkable recovery through conservation efforts. These massive herbivores play a crucial role in shaping the ecosystems they inhabit and are culturally significant to many Indigenous peoples.

Coyote

Canis latrans

The coyote is a highly adaptable canid found throughout North America, from deserts and grasslands to forests and urban areas. Known for their intelligence and resourcefulness, coyotes are skilled hunters and scavengers, preying on small mammals, birds, and insects. Despite being persecuted by humans, coyotes have thrived in a variety of landscapes and are considered an integral part of North American ecosystems.

Mountain Lion

Puma concolor

The mountain lion, also known as cougar or puma, is the largest wildcat species in North America. Found in diverse habitats ranging from mountains and forests to deserts and swamps, mountain lions are solitary and elusive predators. With powerful limbs and keen senses, they are skilled hunters, preying on deer, elk, and other ungulates. Despite their adaptability, mountain lions face habitat loss and fragmentation, as well as conflicts with humans.

Wildlife of South America

Jaguar

Panthera onca

The jaguar is the largest big cat species in the Americas and is found primarily in the rainforests of Central and South America, as well as in parts of Mexico and the southwestern United States. Known for its powerful build and distinctive rosette patterns on its fur, the jaguar is an apex predator that hunts a wide range of prey, including deer, capybaras, and caimans. Despite being highly elusive, jaguars play a crucial role in maintaining the balance of their ecosystems.

Sloth

Folivora

Sloths are arboreal mammals found in the rainforests of Central and South America. Known for their slow-moving behavior and unique adaptations, sloths spend most of their lives hanging upside down from trees, feeding on leaves and buds. With their gentle demeanor and enigmatic charm, sloths have captured the fascination of people around the world, becoming popular symbols of tropical biodiversity.

Tapir

Tapirus species

Tapirs are large, herbivorous mammals native to the forests and grasslands of Central and South America, as well as Southeast Asia. With their distinctive trunk-like snouts, tapirs are browsers, feeding on leaves, fruits, and aquatic vegetation. Despite their seemingly ungainly appearance, tapirs are excellent swimmers and play an important role in seed dispersal and ecosystem health.

Red-eyed Tree Frog

Agalychnis callidryas

The red-eyed tree frog is an iconic amphibian species found in the rainforests of Central America and northern South America. Known for its vibrant colors and distinctive red eyes, this arboreal frog spends much of its time in the treetops, hunting insects and avoiding predators. The red-eyed tree frog has become a symbol of tropical rainforests and is popular in the pet trade and eco-tourism.

Red piranha

Pygocentrus nattereri

Piranhas are a group of carnivorous fish found in the rivers and lakes of South America, known for their sharp teeth and predatory behavior. Contrary to popular belief, most piranha species are not aggressive towards humans and typically feed on smaller fish, insects, and carrion. However, they are efficient predators and play a vital role in regulating prey populations and maintaining ecosystem balance.

Iguana iguana

Toucan

Ramphastidae family

Toucans are brightly colored birds native to the tropical forests of Central and South America. Known for their large, colorful bills, toucans feed primarily on fruits, insects, and small reptiles. With their distinctive appearance and raucous calls, toucans are popular symbols of tropical biodiversity and are often featured in art, literature, and cultural celebrations across the Americas.
